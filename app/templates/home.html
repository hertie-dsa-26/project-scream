Diabetes across the United States

The BRFSS 2024 survey captures health data from over 400,000 Americans. Diabetes is one of the most prevalent chronic conditions — and its distribution is far from uniform.

The Southern Belt

States in the South consistently report higher diabetes rates. Historical patterns of poverty, food access, and healthcare coverage all play a role.

Risk factors

BMI, physical activity, smoking, and age are among the strongest predictors. The picture shifts when we look at who is most at risk — not just where they live.
